My motivation in geospatial science stems from my desire to contribute to the resolution of some of the various environmental, public health, and humanitarian problems experienced in Nigeria; the country's socioeconomic indices; and my conviction that the principle of nudge can be best accomplished by including the "dimension of place/where" and "cartography". As a GIS analyst, I am familiar with the analysis and workflow used to leverage spatial models and earth observation techniques for improving and innovating projects.


I am a multi-skilled and data-driven consultant with 3+ years of experience spanning client support, data analysis, resource allocation and management, sustainable development initiative, and information technology, particularly geospatial data science who is research-focused and innovative-driven and believes in the art of engagement in creating a competitive advantage for organizations and designing effective enterprise solutions. I am a strong believer in the power of digital, data-driven technologies to rendering services to make the world a better place and to build stronger communities and relationships. I am interested in Geo Business Innovation, Data Strategy and Insight Management since completing the Copernicus Accelerator 2020 Start-up program.


Futhermore, my wide range of experience has provided me with a strong acumen to proactively recognize problems, deliver innovative strategies, and influence positive business results. I am a fast learner and results-oriented with a demonstrated passion for building relationships, cultivating partnerships, building resilient communities, and promoting informed decision-making. I am self-motivated, reliable, and adaptable with strong attention to detail, who is constantly looking for new challenges and opportunities, and eager to learn about new technologies.

A case study in Paradise Town, Butte County – California

Spatiotemporal Assessment of Particulate Matter 2.5 (PM2.5) in Lagos State, Nigeria

This project looks at how to evaluate and estimate the spatio-temporal distribution of ozone using geostatistical method (using both inverse distance weighting and kriging methods) in Lagos - Nigeria from 2007 to 2016.
